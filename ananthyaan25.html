Themes

Track 1:
Electric Vehicle Two-Wheeler Design

1A. On/Off-Road Sports Vehicle

  • Design an electric sports bike capable of handling both urban streets and off-road terrains.
  • Key focus: High torque motors, rugged suspension systems, regenerative braking, and fast charging.
  • Considerations: Lightweight frame materials, aerodynamic enhancements, and smart energy management.

1B. Family Vehicle with Luggage Baskets

  • Develop a practical and ergonomic EV two-wheeler for families, ensuring comfort, safety, and storage efficiency.
  • Key focus: Extended range, comfortable seating, load-bearing capacity, and modular luggage compartments.
  • Considerations: Child safety, center-of-gravity optimization, and smart connectivity features.

1C. Self-Balanced Two-Wheeler

  • Design a futuristic self-balancing electric two-wheeler using gyroscopic stabilization or AI-assisted control.
  • Key focus: Enhanced stability, urban mobility solutions, and rider-friendly automation.
  • Considerations: AI-assisted control algorithms, auto-park features, and accessibility for all age groups.

Track 2:
EV Software Wars - AI, Automation & Digital Twins

2A. AI for EV Optimization

  • Develop AI-driven solutions for battery management, energy efficiency, and adaptive power delivery.
  • Use machine learning models for predictive analytics in battery life extension and route optimization.

2B. EV Automation & Autonomy

  • Design intelligent automation for electric vehicles, including self-driving assistance, automated diagnostics, and fleet management.
  • Explore V2X (Vehicle-to-Everything) communication and AI-based traffic predictions.

2C. Digital Twins for EV Development

  • Implement digital twin technology to simulate EV behavior, test components virtually, and optimize performance before physical prototyping.
  • Use real-time data integration for predictive maintenance and AI-driven decision-making.

About VisionAstraa EV Academy


The Visionastraa EV Academy, established in collaboration with RV College of Engineering (RVCE), is successfully running intensive, skill-focused training programs at the RVCE campus. With a strong emphasis on hands-on lab sessions and real-world applications, the academy is shaping the next generation of EV industry professionals. Designed for B.Tech, MTech graduates and Diploma holders, the 4-month immersive training program provides in-depth knowledge across multiple domains of electric vehicle technology, ensuring participants gain the expertise needed to excel in the rapidly evolving electric mobility sector.

About VTU


Visvesvaraya Technological University (VTU), headquartered in Belagavi, Karnataka, is one of India's premier technical universities, named after Sir M. Visvesvaraya. With 200+ affiliated colleges and a strong focus on engineering, research, and innovation, VTU plays a key role in shaping industry-ready professionals. The university emphasizes cutting-edge technologies, including electric mobility, AI, IoT, and Industry 4.0, through advanced curriculum and industry collaborations.

Team Size & Eligibility


Eligibility


  • Open to students from all years (1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th year).
  • Participants from any engineering or technology discipline can apply.
  • Both department-specific and interdisciplinary teams are allowed (interdisciplinary teams are encouraged).

Team Formation


  • Each team must have a minimum of 2 members and a maximum of 4 members.
  • A participant cannot be part of multiple teams.
  • Teams must ensure that all members are officially registered before the deadline

Registration Process


Online Registration


  • Teams must register through the official ANANTHYAAN 2025 portal.
  • Each team must provide details such as team name, member details (names, year, department, email, and contact number).

Project Proposal Submission


  • Teams need to submit a brief abstract (max 300 words) outlining their chosen track and initial concept.
  • The proposal should highlight the problem statement, innovative approach, and expected outcomes.

Confirmation & Selection


  • Shortlisted teams will receive a confirmation email with further instructions.
  • Only selected teams will proceed to the hackathon phase.

Important Rules


  • No pre-built prototypes allowed; all work must be original and developed during the hackathon.
  • Teams must adhere to competition guidelines and deadlines.
  • All participants must carry a valid college ID during the event.

Facilities provided at the venue


  • Internet Connectivity and reliable power supply will be provided.
  • Food, snacks, and refreshments will be available for all participants.
  • Separate rest areas will be arranged for boys and girls.
  • First Aid facilities will be available on-site.
  • Teams must bring all necessary components and software required for developing their product/prototype.

Deadlines
